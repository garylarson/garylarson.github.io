Teaching

During grad school I was a TA for a number of courses and taught a summer session of our department’s version of statistics 101 for undergrads. Extra special thanks to Mine Çetinkaya-Rundel and Nicole Dalzell for their generous help putting together materials for my Summer 2015 session!

Fall 2015: TA for Advanced Statistical Computing (STA 863), taught by Prof. Scott Schmidler.

Summer 2015: instructor of record for Data Analysis and Statistical Inference (STA 101). Gave daily lectures, twice-weekly labs, and performed all other duties related to the course. Click here to see my course’s homepage.

Spring 2015: part-time TA for Statistical Consulting Workshop (STA 851). Reviewed draft client reports; some grading.

Fall 2014: Community TA another iteration of the Coursera course Data Analysis and Statistical Inference mentioned below.

Spring 2014: I served as a Community TA and helped with materials preparation for the Coursera course Data Analysis and Statistical Inference. The course was designed and taught by Duke Statistical Science faculty member Mine Çetinkaya-Rundel.

Fall 2013: co-TA for Linear Models (STA 721) taught by Merlise Clyde. Held weekly office hours; graded homework assignments and final exams.

Spring 2013: TA for Data Analysis and Statistical Inference (STA 101) taught by Mine Çetinkaya-Rundel. Taught weekly lab sessions; held weekly office hours; grading.
