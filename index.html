Hi! This website hosts some info on my PhD research as well as a technical-type blog where I post about statistics, data science, and related computing issues. This site is under major construction as of August 2018, as I migrate sites. For best results, see the site I’m migrating from.

I am PhD statistician and data scientist. I have extensive experience in Bayesian modeling and am comfortable working with a wide variety of statistical models. I did my PhD in statistics at Duke’s Department of Statistical Science, advised by Scott Schmidler. Before grad school I worked in the actuarial profession. From 2007-2010 I worked as an actuarial analyst in Charlotte, NC. For a year after that, I worked as a pension benefits consultant in Shanghai, China where I helped multinational corporations build retirement benefits packages for their China-bound expat employees.
